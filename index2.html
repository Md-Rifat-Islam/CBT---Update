The Bell 212 (also known as the Twin Two-Twelve) is a two-blade, twin-engine, medium helicopter that first flew in 1968. Originally manufactured by Bell Helicopter in Fort Worth, Texas, United States, production was moved to Mirabel, Quebec, Canada in 1988, along with all Bell commercial helicopter production after that plant opened in 1986.

Trades

Engine

The Bell 212 is powered by a Pratt & Whitney Canada PT6T-3 Twin-Pac engine.

AirFrame

Based on the stretched fuselage Bell 205, the Bell 212 was originally developed as the CUH-1N.

Electric Components

During single engine failure the remaining engine can deliver 900 shp (671 kW) for 30 minutes, or 765 shp (571 kW) continuously.

Instruments

Pratt & Whitney Canada PT6T-3 Twin-Pac made up of two coupled PT6 power turbines driving a common gearbox.

Radio

Global Position System. Radar-Altimeter. VHF Radio. Autopilot. RMI / RNAV. Audiosystem. UHF NAV.

Armament

Systems used for offensive and defensive purposes and make use of a wide variety of weapon types including machine guns and rockets.
